What is a Backlog?

In the gaming world, a "backlog" is a collection of games you own but haven't finished—or sometimes haven't even started. Whether it's from a Steam sale or a subscription service, these games pile up, waiting for their turn in the spotlight.

Why Manage It?

Managing your backlog reduces "decision paralysis." Instead of spending your limited free time staring at a list of 100 titles, a tracker helps you focus on what actually excites you right now, saving you time and mental energy.

Deciding What's Next

  • Follow the Mood: Don't play a 100-hour RPG if you're tired; pick an indie "palate cleanser" instead.
  • Check the Time: Use sites like HowLongToBeat to match a game to your schedule.
  • The Rule of Three: Keep only three games active: one big, one small, and one endless (like a sports or sim game).
